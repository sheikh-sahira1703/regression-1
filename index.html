Introduction to univariate linear regression

Introduction to Bivariate data

Bivariate data is a form of data in which one data point is linked to another data point and the order of arrangement matters Eg – height and weight In this example, the order of insertion of data matters as a particular person of a particular height has a particular weight and since the order of data insertion matters as we cannot assign another person’s weight to a particular person of a particular height

Descriptive statistics related to bivariate data

Mean:

We can represent the mean of bivariate data as a tuple containing the mean of individual data columns.

Median

We cannot represent the median of bivariate data as a tuple containing the median of individual data columns as while finding the median of individual data columns, the order of insertion matters and it is possible that while arranging the individual columns, the bivariate data as whole might get shuffled

Standard Deviation

We can represent the standard deviation of the bivariate dataset as a tuple of standard deviation of the individual columns.


Conclusion

Any descriptive statistic which does not require ordering of data points in order to calculate it can be expressed as a tuple containing that descriptive statistic of individual columns.

Correlation

It measures the degree of association of y with x
It is obtained by dividing the covariance by the standard deviation of x multiplied by the standard deviation of y

We divide by the standard deviation of x multiplied by the standard deviation of y because:

  1. It cancels off the units which were there in the covariance
  2. Standard deviation is always positive that is why it does not affect the sign
  3. It brings the quantity in the range of {-1,1} which provides a universal scale to compare how strong or weak the correlation is
